INGLÉS STORY Osteoporosis is a big health worry. It mostly affects women who can't have babies anymore. It makes their bones break more easily. The U.S. Preventive Services Task Force (USPSTF) gave advice on how to check for this in 2018. 1. The USPSTF says to use a special X-ray, called DEXA, to check bone strength in women who are 65 or older. 2. Women who can't have babies anymore but are younger than 65 should also be checked. This is if they might break a bone as easily as a 65-year-old white woman with no extra risks. The FRAX calculator can help decide this. 3. The USPSTF doesn't have enough proof to say men should be checked all the time for osteoporosis. 4. The advice says to think about each person's risks. These can be things like being very thin, smoking, family history, or having broken bones before. 5. Things like eating enough calcium and vitamin D, doing exercise that puts weight on your bones, and not smoking are good for bones. Doctors should check women over 65 for osteoporosis. They should also think about checking younger women if they might break a bone easily. It's important to talk about the good and bad parts of checking and treating.
