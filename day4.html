INGLÉS STORY Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is a common illness that greatly affects a person's health. The KDIGO 2024 guidelines show the value of two types of drugs, SGLT2 inhibitors (SGLT2i) and GLP-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1RA), in managing CKD for different risk levels. 1. SGLT2 inhibitors are good for CKD patients, even those without diabetes. They help protect the kidneys and slow down the disease. 2. GLP-1 receptor agonists are suggested for CKD patients with type 2 diabetes. They provide heart benefits and may protect the kidneys. 3. The guidelines stress the need for treatment plans that are tailored to the patient. Things like other illnesses and risk of side effects should be considered. 4. Both types of drugs have proven to lessen albuminuria and keep the kidneys working well in clinical trials. 5. Using these drugs in managing CKD is in line with the move towards early action and full risk reduction plans. 6. Doctors should watch out for possible side effects and change treatments if needed to get the best results for the patient.
