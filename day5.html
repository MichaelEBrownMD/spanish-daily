INGLÉS STORY In 2024–2025, RSV vaccine is mainly for adults who get the most benefit. ACIP June 2024 gives more detail on who and when to vaccinate for adults ≥60 years. 1. Clear benefit: Adults ≥75 years, and adults 60–74 years with at least one major risk condition, have the best evidence for benefit from one dose of RSV vaccine. Major risk conditions include chronic lung or heart disease, moderate–severe immunocompromise, advanced kidney or liver disease, diabetes with end-organ damage, some neurologic conditions, or living in nursing homes. Use shared clinical decision-making. 2. Healthy adults 60–74 years: For immunocompetent adults 60–74 years without these comorbidities, ACIP says use shared clinical decision-making. Absolute risk reduction is small, and cost-effectiveness is less good. 3. Timing: Give vaccine just before RSV season. In most of the continental U.S., this is late summer to early fall (August–October). 4. Durability: Arexvy and Abrysvo show waning antibody titers but good clinical efficacy into at least a second RSV season. ACIP does not advise routine revaccination now. 5. Safety: Most adverse events are mild or moderate. Rare neurologic events like GBS can occur.
