It helps to reduce weight and control blood sugar. Studies show it also helps the heart and kidneys. It is a good choice for patients who are overweight and have CKD. 2. There is another medicine called tirzepatide. It works on two things: glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP) and GLP-1 receptors. It is better than GLP-1RAs alone in reducing weight and controlling blood sugar. It can be used in patients who need to lose more weight. 3. Sodium-glucose cotransporter-2 inhibitors (SGLT2i) are known to help the kidneys and heart. They are very good for patients with CKD. They slow down the disease and lower the risk of heart failure. 4. A good plan for kidney and metabolic management uses medicines that help both metabolism and organs. This is in line with ADA’s goals. 5. When choosing medicines, we can start with GLP-1RAs or tirzepatide for weight and blood sugar control. Then, we can add SGLT2i for more help with the kidneys and heart.
