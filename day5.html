INGLÉS STORY Metabolic surgery is now a standard treatment for severe obesity and type 2 diabetes in adolescents. It often works better than intensive lifestyle or pharmacologic therapy. New data and guidelines say: send patients earlier, before obesity-related comorbidities get worse. Teen-LABS long-term follow-up (Inge et al., N Engl J Med 2016; N Engl J Med 2019) shows BMI stays about 26–29% lower for up to 8 years. Many have remission of type 2 diabetes (up to about 80%), hypertension, and dyslipidemia. Adolescents have similar or better remission of diabetes and hypertension after Roux-en-Y gastric bypass than matched adults, and less persistent diabetes (Inge et al., N Engl J Med 2019). Early metabolic surgery in youth with recent-onset type 2 diabetes gives higher remission and less microvascular disease than late surgery (Inge et al., N Engl J Med 2019; TODAY study). American Academy of Pediatrics 2023 and ASMBS/IFSO 2022 say: consider surgery for class II obesity with serious comorbidity, or class III obesity, as early as you can. Sleeve gastrectomy is now most common. Perioperative mortality is very low (<0.3%), but long-term follow-up is needed for micronutrient deficiencies, bone health, mental health, and alcohol or substance misuse.
