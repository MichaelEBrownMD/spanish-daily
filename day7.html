INGLÉS STORY Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) is still a main cause of infant hospitalization. Risk is highest in the first 6 months of life. In 2023–2024, maternal RSV vaccination (Abrysvo, Pfizer) and infant nirsevimab changed prevention plans. ACIP updated guidance in 2024. ACIP says: use EITHER maternal RSV vaccination with Abrysvo at 32–36 weeks’ gestation (seasonal, per place) OR infant nirsevimab for most infants, but not both in routine care. Abrysvo is a single-dose bivalent prefusion F vaccine. In a phase 3 trial, maternal vaccination lowered medically attended severe RSV LRTI in infants by about 70–80% in the first 3 months of life. Best timing is 32–36 weeks’ gestation. This helps transplacental antibody transfer and may lower preterm birth risk seen with earlier dosing. If an infant is born less than 14 days after Abrysvo, still give nirsevimab. If Abrysvo was given 14 days or more before birth, do not give nirsevimab, except in rare high-risk cases. Maternal vaccine antibodies protect about 3–6 months. Nirsevimab protects for one RSV season.
